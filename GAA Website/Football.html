THE SENIOR FOOTBALL CHAMPIONSHIP

The senior football Championship is the focal point of the GAA calender. The competition culminates in the All Ireland final hosted in Croke Park.

History

The tournament has taken place every year since 1887, except in 1888 when the competition was not played due to a tour of the United States by would-be competitors. The winning team receives the Sam Maguire Cup.

The final has been played at Croke Park in Dublin since 1913, with the exception of the 1947 final which was played at the Polo Grounds in New York. Finals held before the GAA's acquirement of Croke Park were played at venues around Dublin and the counties of Cork, Kildare and Tipperary.


Past Winners

Over the last 20 years there have been some phenominal teams which have dominated the spoils at the top of intercounty football. Dublin, Kerry and Tyrone have been the most dominating , winning 15 of the last 20 All Irelands
